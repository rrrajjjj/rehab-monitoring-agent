### Recommendation Algorithm

1. **Learning Rate (ΔDM)**

   * Each exercise has adjustable settings—like target size or number of tasks—that make it easier or harder called difficulty modulators (DMs).
   * After every session, the system measures how much those settings had to change to keep the exercise challenging. That change is **delta-DM**.
   * If delta-DM stays around zero for a few sessions (no more need to adjust), it means the patient has **plateaued** on that exercise.

2. **Engagement (Adherence)**

   * This is simply the share of prescribed sessions the patient completes.
   * High adherence means the patient is sticking with the plan; low adherence may signal fatigue or lack of motivation.

3. **Skill Match (Competence or Patient–Protocol Fit)**

   * The system compares each patient’s clinical scores (e.g., MoCA for cognition, ARAT for arm function) to the known goals of an exercise (which motor or thinking skills it trains).
   * If an exercise targets the areas your patient needs most, and their scores show they’re ready for it, that exercise gets a high **fit** rating. If not, it scores lower.

4. **Putting It All Together**

   * For every exercise, the CDSS looks at:

     1. **ΔDM** (is the patient still learning?)
     2. **Adherence** (are they doing it?)
     3. **Fit** (does it match their clinical needs?)
   * It ranks exercises by combining these three factors into one simple priority list.

5. **When to Switch Exercises**

   * If an exercise’s delta-DM has plateaued and another exercise is showing better learning or match, the system suggests a swap. It can also happen when the adherence falls too low indicating the user skipping the protocol frequently. 
   * You always remain in control: accept, adjust, or reject any suggestion.

# 1. Introduction

## 1.1 Intended purpose

The Medical Information Management System (MIMS) is a web platform for health professionals to access and manage patient data. The MIMS integrates the patient data collected by the various components of the RGS (Rehabilitation Gaming System) Ecosystem, such as RGSclinic, RGSapp, and RGSwear. Moreover, through the MIMS, healthcare professionals can access various reports, including physical activity metrics, joint range of motion assessments, and treatment adherence. Health professionals, such as neurologists, physical therapists, and occupational therapists, will monitor the patient's performance and prescribe the training activities.

## 2. Intended purpose {#intended-purpose}

The RGS+ AI system is designed to support and enhance post-stroke rehabilitation by assisting clinicians in making more patient-centered and data-driven treatment planning.

The AI module analyzes patient performance data to provide predictive modeling and therapy personalization suggestions. These outputs are intended for use only by qualified healthcare professionals, who retain full authority over treatment decisions.

**The AI does not override or replace clinical judgment and is explicitly not intended as a primary diagnostic or therapeutic decision-making system.** Clinical supervision remains paramount, ensuring that all AI-driven insights are carefully reviewed and validated by healthcare professionals.

---



# 1. Intended User {#intended-user}

Health professionals, such as neurologists, physical therapists, and occupational therapists, will monitor the patient's performance and prescribe the training activities, while having access to all the patient data and use the system as a decision-making support.


# 1. Getting started with MIMS {#getting-started-with-mims}

Health professionals will manage patient data through the Medical Information Management System (MIMS), a web platform that integrates the patient data collected by RGS with the prescription of exercises, reports, and the prognostics models of the Artificial Intelligence module.

All this information is integrated, processed, and presented via the application MIMS to enhance decision-making for the clinical staff.
