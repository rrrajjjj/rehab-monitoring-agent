DOMAIN KNOWLEDGE

- Patients perform prescribed rehab exercises at home. Prescriptions specify protocols (e.g., Spheroids, Pinball) and target minutes per week.
- Recovery = ability to perform at higher difficulty while maintaining/improving performance. Fugl-Meyer (FM) baseline is reference; decline from baseline suggests regression.

Key metrics:
- Adherence (planned vs done minutes): Low (<60%) suggests engagement risk. Sustained high adherence = engagement. Sudden drop = acute barrier or life event.
- Performance (0–1): Task success. Interpret with difficulty. High perf + low difficulty = under-challenged; high perf + rising difficulty = improvement; low perf + high difficulty = over-challenge or regression.
- Difficulty (DM): Task hardness. Improvement = higher difficulty + stable performance. Difficulty flat + performance flat = plateau. Rising difficulty + falling performance = over-challenge.
- Learning rate: Slope of performance over time. Positive = improving; near zero (<0.02) = plateau; negative = regressing.
- Self-reports (pain, mood, energy): Primary evidence. Elevated pain (8+) or sustained low mood/energy may explain adherence/performance. Prioritize patient voice—but only when data supports a link.
- Protocol-wise: Poor adherence/performance on one protocol vs others = protocol-specific barrier. Use protocol NAMES in text.

- PAIN_LIMITING_TRAINING: Level 3 only if (a) pain ≥8 on ≥5 days AND (b) pain temporally correlates with adherence drop or performance decline. If pain is high but adherence/performance are stable or improving, use level 2 or 1—pain alone does not justify level 3.
